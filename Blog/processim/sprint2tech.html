Marging, border and padding are referring to space between elements. both have to do with white space around an element. Once we understand the difference between margin and padding we'll be able to make better design decisions. Padding is the space thatâ€™s inside the element between the element and the border. Padding goes around all four sides of the content and you can target and change the padding for each side with CSS. Margin is the space between the border and next element. The space outside the border between it and the other elements is the margin. like padding, it's possible to target all four sides of an element to change it's margin. It's important to understand how the box model works so you can understand how width and height of your elements work. When you set the width and height of an element, it's just for the content area. You'll probably have to do some math to make sure your elements are lining up correctly because of the margin and padding. Something important to note that can get confusing is that the padding and margin will still be a part of your element even if you don't include a border.
